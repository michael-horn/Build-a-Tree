Plants and animals are both eukaryotes (ew-KARR-ee-ohts), which means that they are complex multicellular organism. Plants and animals diverged (evolved into two different forms of life) around 1,400 million years ago.

Birds and lizards diverged much more recently—around 277 million years ago. This means that birds and lizards had an ancestor in common more recently than animals and plants.
